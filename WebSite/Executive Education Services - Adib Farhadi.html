Adib Farhadi Ph.D. is a trade and development economist with over fifteen years of experience in academia, government and private sector.  Dr. Farhadi is currently an Assistant Professor at University of South Florida (USF) Global Initiative and Fellow at University of Canberra, Institute for Governance & Policy Analysis. Dr. Farhadi most recently served as Visiting Scholar at Johns Hopkins University, School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS) with Professor Frederick Starr which resulted in the seminal publication of Finish the Job: Jumpstart the Afghanistan Economy.   

Dr. Farhadi specializes in emerging, developing and fragile economies with extensive work experience in all aspects of international poverty reduction initiatives, economic growth, private sector development, infrastructure development and international trade.   Dr. Farhadi has advised a variety of international and domestic clients in areas such as economic analysis, strategic analysis and planning, post-conflict stabilization, infrastructure and investment analysis.  

​Dr. Farhadi earned his B.S. Degree at East Carolina University in 1994, his Master Degree at New York University in 1996 and his Ph.D. in economy from University of Canberra in 2015.
